The problem we address

In many organizations, the problem is not the decision itself,
but what happens after it.

Who made the decision
Which criteria were used
Why the outcome occurred

When these are left to memory or interpretation, responsibility drifts and outcomes become unstable.

Sizlon fixes this structurally.

Our principles

We do not judge The system does not issue conclusions.
We do not recommend The system does not direct choice.
We do not make decisions Decision-making remains with the user.
We do not interpret results Systems apply predefined rules and record results.

Responsibility structure

Sizlon systems are designed not to perform judgment. They apply criteria and record results. Interpretation and final decisions remain with the user or organization.
